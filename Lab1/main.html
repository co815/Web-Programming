Common Cyber Threats

Phishing Attacks

Phishing is a social engineering attack where criminals disguise themselves as trustworthy entities to steal sensitive information such as passwords and credit card numbers. Always verify the sender before clicking any link.


Ransomware

Ransomware encrypts a victim's files and demands payment for the decryption key. High-profile attacks like WannaCry and NotPetya caused billions in damages worldwide. Regular backups are your best defence.


Man-in-the-Middle (MitM)

In a MitM attack, an adversary secretly intercepts and possibly alters communications between two parties who believe they are talking directly to each other. Use HTTPS and VPNs to mitigate this risk.

Cybersecurity Best Practices

  • Use strong, unique passwords for every account — consider a password manager.
  • Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) wherever possible.
  • Keep your operating system and software updated and patched.
  • Avoid connecting to public Wi-Fi without a VPN.
  • Back up your data regularly using the 3-2-1 rule (3 copies, 2 media, 1 offsite).
  • Be sceptical of unsolicited emails, calls, and messages — think before you click.

Did you know? A cyberattack occurs every 39 seconds on average, affecting one in three Americans each year. (University of Maryland study)
